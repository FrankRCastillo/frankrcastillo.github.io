Frank R. Castillo

I am a family man, student, and former United States Marine. I currently attend the University of South Florida and hope to apply to the university’s PhD in economics by the end of 2017. When time allows, I also write about politics, economics, international relations, and technology. I love to think about important issues, as well as have conversations with others who do the same. I seek constant intellectual growth, and I live my life by the mantra that I am willing to have my ideas and preconceptions demolished so long as you bring a proper wrecking ball.

Professionally, I’ve had the opportunity to work in fascinating areas, including Anti-Money Laundering (AML), financial compliance, and information technology. During a stint as an AML analyst for a major financial institution, I saw how necessary it was for banks to leverage technology to help mitigate the risks posed by financial crime, which inspired my current academic interests. My hope is to someday integrate my knowledge of big data with research on behavioral economics to develop systems that help prevent fraud and money laundering.

My adult life began on June 28th, 2004, when I stepped on the yellow footprints of Marine Corps Recruit Depot Parris Island, North Carolina. A year before, my phone rang and, with the most charisma and enthusiasm I had ever heard be transmitted over a phone line, Sergeant Davis asked if I was interested in discussing my future. “Sure, why not?” Being 17 and not knowing much of anything, I jumped in. Nine years later, I had been to Iraq, floated around the Pacific and Indian oceans aboard a Marine Expeditionary Unit, and saw enough of the world to convince me to become its eternal student. I left service in 2013 with the rank of Staff Sergeant.
